Narrative

Jacob Teeter was born about 1762 in Germany, and emigrated in 1780 from
Germany to America, along with a companion Martin Widenhouse [1,2].
Jacob was only 17 or 18 years old at the time. They were stowaways on an ocean vessel for a stormy voyage of 6 months, and finally landed in Charleston, South Carolina, where there was a large German settlement. A Mr. Wilhelm, a German immigrant who had settled in North Carolina near Buffalo Creek, had travelled as usual for supplies to Charleston, and there met the young men Teeter and Widenhouse. He returned home with them to nowadays Cabarrus County, where they worked for him until they themselves were able to buy land and start their own families. Jacob Teeter married and had six children: Samuel (1771), Catherine, Jacob, George (1785), Peter (1787), and Elizabeth.
Catherine married Jacob Harkey, Sr. George married Katharine Deberry. Peter Teeter married Elizabeth Dry in 1809 and they had sons George H., Archibald Monroe, and Marten A. George H. married Elizabeth Petry in 1830, Archibald Monroe married Rebecca Sossman in 1835, and Marten A. married Sally Petry in 1830. Elizabeth married Peter Dry in 1809. It is not known when the elder Jacob Teeter died.[Jacob Teeter.FTW]
